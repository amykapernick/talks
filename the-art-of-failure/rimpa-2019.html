Learning from Failure
- In development we use a particular type of software called git for version control
- It tracks any changes that we make
- And allows us to easily back our code up online when we don't have cloud storage or a network drive
- But unfortunately you do actually have to manually push your code to the cloud to back it up
- And that's not something I'm great at doing
- And one day, my computer died
- It was slow, so I tried to turn it off and on again
- Because that's how you fix everything
- Except it wouldn't turn back on
- And I lost several days worth of work
- I told you I wasn't great at pushing my code
- Did I mention that this was 2 hours before a client meeting
- And I now didn't have any of the work I'd done for him?
- Thankfully the client was also a developer
- And understood my pain
- But it also gave me a chance to learn a very important lesson
- Not only am I (slightly) better at pushing my code changes so I have them backed up
- But I set up an automatic backup that is running all the time, and backs up my entire computer (just in case)

Manage Your Ducks

- And on a similar note, make sure you're managing your ducks
- That's right, I said ducks
- But to clarify, I'm not talking about these ducks
- I'm talking about these ducks

About how I allocate my ducks.

If you have a limited number of ducks to give om your lifetime, you need to be careful where you're spending them

So say for example you can give up to 10 ducks about a particular situation

- Say there's something you care a little bit about
- Like where you're going for dinner
- You might choose to give that decision 3 ducks
- And maybe there's something you care a lot about
- Like who you're going to spend the rest of your life with
- Or maybe there's something you don't really care about
- And you just don't have any ducks to give at all
- So I started thinking about how I allocate my ducks
- Am I giving enough ducks where it counts?
- Am I giving too many ducks on something that isn't important or to someone who doesn't deserve them?
- While we don't get to choose everything in our life
- We always choose where we're giving our ducks
- If you give too many ducks on things that aren't important
- You could run out of ducks to give
- But on the other hand, if you're too stingy with your ducks
- You may end up with a lot of ducks and nothing important to give them to
